Grants

Thank you to the following organisations and government agencies for helping us to create optimism.

Australian Government

Department of Infrastructure, Transport, Cities and Regional Development (2019)

With thanks to the support of our local Federal Member Dave Sharma (Member for Wentworth), we have been successful in attaining $15,000 Stronger Communities Grant for our Wright of Passage project.

The funding will be used to purchase and install critical small capital equipment and upgrades to enable the continued restoration of the wooden yacht Wright of Passage Mercator by young trainees (16-25 yo) who are challenged by learning and life.

The award of this grant will contribute to purchasing the resources and equipment upgrades required for the next group of trainees. For instance - specialist tools, a work bench, fixtures, fittings, a replacement fuel tank, replacements to the standing rigging, and professional labour to work with our youth to install the fuel tank and standing rigging.

Overall, the Stronger Communities Grant funding will assist in developing confidence and life skills our youth participants need in order to seek employment and/or further formal training or education.

Queensland Government

Gambling Community Benefit Fund (2019)

The Queensland Government, Office of Liquor and Gaming Regulation, has awarded SWD with a $25,000 grant to help us cover the expenses of the 2019 Winds of Joy Queensland campaign.

We thank the Queensland Government for investing in their community.

The Coffs Harbour Clubs

ClubGRANTS (2019)

SWD has been awarded with $4000 from the Coffs Harbour Clubs, ClubGRANTS, to fund a week of Winds of Joy sailing programs in Coffs Harbour.

We thank the Coffs Harbour Clubs for investing in their community.

Club Liverpool

Club Grants (2019)

Club Liverpool provides grants to a range of community groups and charities.

SWD was successful in its application to Club Liverpool and received a grant to increase the number of people with a disability from the Liverpool area accessing the Winds of Joy program.

NDIS

ILC Grants (2019)

Information, Linkages and Capacity Building (ILC) is all about community inclusion. It's about creating connections between people with a disability and the communities they live in.

SWD is using the ILC Grant award to fund two projects. Firstly, an Organisational Capacity Project, to develop a holistic open source national data management platform. Secondly, an Individual Capacity Project, to develop the sustainability of the innovative Wright of Passage program for young people with disabilities based in Sydney, by employing a Project Coordinator to work with stakeholders to document and implement the program pathway for future years, including a train the trainer approach to upskill participants to be the mentors for young people on other on-water programs.

Port Waratah Coal Services

Community Investment Funding (2019)

Port Waratah Coal Services has awarded SWD with a $5000 grant for 10 Winds of Joy sails for the Newcastle community.

Thanks also to SWD volunteer from Newcastle, Scott (Parko) Parkinson for his support in applying for this grant.

Office of Sport

Local Sport Grant Program (2018 - 2019)

This $2000 grant will be used for SWD Programs in 2018 up to mid 2019 and is to provide sailing experiences for people with disabilities or at a disadvantage.

Arbonne Charitable Foundation (2018)

October / November 2018

The Arbonne Charitable Foundation funded $3700 towards training video costs and on water equipment for Winds of Change with Dulwich Hill School for Visual Arts & Design.

Carers NSW

National Carers Week (2018 & 2019)

The NSW Department of Family and Community Services funded Carers NSW to provide a grant to SWD. This grant was to contribute to providing a Winds of Care sail during National Carers Week in 2018 and 2019.

Hon Ray Williams MP (2018)

NSW Minister for Multiculturalism and NSW Minister for Disability Services

$2000 for wet weather safety equipment for Wright of Passage Youth participants.

The Marian and E.H. Flack Trust (2017 & 2018)

SWD was successful in receiving a grant for "Services to disadvantaged groups or organisations". Providing services that directly support children, adolescents, adults, and families who are disadvantaged due to education, financial, and/or social circumstances.
