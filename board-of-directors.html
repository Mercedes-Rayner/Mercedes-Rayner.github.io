Our Board of Directors

Andrew King

President

Andrew King has moved into the SWD board role of President. Andrew has extensive business experience in a broad range of manufacturing and distribution businesses. He has superior communication skills, a passion for brands and organisations.

Andrew also brings a broad knowledge of the management of risk and an enhanced ability to analyse complex situations, which is helpful for SWD.

His most recent roles and directorships include: Foresight’s Global Coaching, Executive Coach Capstone Partners, Consultant NSW Manufacturing Task Force, Chair Shore School, Member of Council, Honorary Treasurer.

David Pescud

Vice President and Founder

David is dyslexic. He can't read or write and left school at 15. Yet he retired in his early 40s to devote his life to his first love, sailing. More particularly, to devote himself to creating the opportunity for disabled people, especially kids, to learn to sail.

David founded SWD in 1994, when he pulled together the first crew of disabled sailors to enter the 50th anniversary Sydney to Hobart Yacht Race. SWD is about to enter its 20th year.

David is a man on a mission to show that disabled people can take their place alongside the able-bodied, "ABs" as he calls them, and have their abilities acknowledged ahead of their disabilities.

David won the NSW Disability Industry Innovation Award for Distinguished Service in 2013, recognising his contributions over the last two decades.

In 2014, David Pescud won the NSW and then the National the Pride Of Australia Medal for Inspiration nominated by a crew member for his mentoring. He also won a Distinguished Long Service Award 2016 from Sports NSW.

Peter Steel

Treasurer

Peter has been an active member and volunteer of SWD for the last year, involving himself in both on water and off water activities as well as Trainee Co-Ordinator for the Wright of Passage program.

He has a wide breadth of business experience in a range of manufacturing and logistic businesses as well as in key finance roles. Peter has strong people and stakeholders management skills, experience in transformation work and understands the importance of brands.

His most recent roles before retiring, included Coca-Cola Amatil and Toll Holdings where Peter worked in senior finance and strategy roles.

John (Jack) Gray

Secretary

John Gray has a lifelong association with sailing which is ongoing. He is a partner of a New South Wales and Queensland Law Practice where he practises commercial law and currently sits on several private boards.

He has been involved for many years in a voluntary capacity in sporting and community clubs and associations.

Current involvement is with the North Bondi RSL sub-branch and the Infants Home Ashfield. Through his association with the Infants Home Ashfield, which services over 2,500 children each year, many of whom are at risk and the Bondi Veterans Centre which provides services, in particular to ex-servicemen in psychological and logistical need, he has an appreciation of the benefits that programs of the type run by SWD can provide.

James Hunter

Board Member

James is blind. He lost his eyesight in 2015 and is now committed to open the eyes of others to what might be possible with a positive mind-set, determination and the support of good people.

James has been an active member and volunteer of SWD for the last three years, involving himself in both on water and off water activities.

Before retiring, James worked at the University of New England for 17 years in a number of roles relating to partnership and business development and held academic positions in strategic and general management.

James has also worked as a Management Consultant specialising in professional practice management issues and as a Corporate Analyst in the banking industry.

Rick Mustillo

Board Member (resigned February 2018)

Rick has been an active member and volunteer of SWD for over five years, involving himself in both on water and off water activities.

Rick is currently Managing Director of both EzyRoll Pty Ltd and Kookaburra Equipment Sales Pty Ltd.

Outside of work and volunteering Rick has been the National President and Board member of Elevating work Platform Association of Australia 2012 -2017 and the NSW President of Elevating Work Platform Association of Australia 2009 – 2012.

Deborah Sandars

Board Member (since May 2019)

Deborah has been a long term on- and off-water volunteer for SWD. She is passionate about creating social change. Deborah brings a work background in early childhood teaching, community development, stakeholder engagement and public sector management.

Deborah is a Fellow of the Institute of Public Administration, has held advisory committee positions at state and national level, and has previously held board positions at Community Child Care and NSW IPAA.

Deborah retired from employment in 2014, enabling her to give time back to the community through a significant commitment to SWD. (Board Member: Jul 15 – Dec 15; Vice President: Dec 15—Dec16) and has been specifically volunteering undertaking the grants role since mid 2017.

Albert Lee

Honorary Board Member

Albert Lee has been involved with SWD since 1994. He has sailed in five Sydney to Hobart races and participated in a number of offshore ocean races. Albert was a team member of the world-record breaking circumnavigation of Australia crew in 2003, and the world-record setting Sydney to Sydney via Lord Howe Island in 2015.

Albert is an optometrist in Rockdale and assists in university clinics at the UNSW. He has a passion for public health and helps in aboriginal medical centres providing eye care. He has also provided aid work in Sri Lanka and Nepal.

Albert has served on a number of professional boards and committees. As a disabled person he will be able to provide perspective to the board and hopes to make a difference.

Marianne Waters

Honorary Board Member

Marianne Waters retired in 2015 after 20 years from the position as Print and Distribution Manager for Reed Business Publishing and Reed Exhibition Companies. The role was to give print and distribution advice and negotiate on behalf Reed Business and Reed Exhibitions for magazine, directories, postage, distribution (and LexisNexis magazines) both within Australia and offshore.

Marianne's previous experience was a hotel owner and manager in country and city hotels and working with country newspapers.Marianne came to know of SWD several years ago and helped facilitate SWD’s logo design and brochure printing. The fabulous logo and new brochures brought SWD to a new level of brand marketing and professional exposure.

Marianne greatly admires SWD efforts in providing change in the lives of people with disabilities and will give whatever help she can to the board.
